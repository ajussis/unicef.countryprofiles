# Egypt — EdTech Suitability & Compliance Profile
A structured, source-based overview to assess how well EdTech tools align with Egypt’s education framework, digital policy environment, regulatory requirements, and delivery realities.

## Requirement Levels (narrative sections)
In sections 1–10, you may still reference whether something is grounded in law, policy, or programme priorities, but do not use colour-based indicators.

### 1. Core Pedagogy
Summary: Egypt's Ministry of Education (MoE) is responsible for the country's core pedagogy. The curriculum is standardized, and it emphasizes the development of critical thinking and skills for the 21st century. The education system is divided into four stages: kindergarten, basic education, secondary education, and higher education.

Requirement Level: This is driven by law and policy

Sources: [Ministry of Education](http://www.moe.gov.eg/english/)

### 2. Focus Skills
Summary: The focus skills in Egypt include literacy, numeracy, and life skills. The curriculum also emphasizes the development of 21st-century skills including critical thinking, creativity, problem-solving, and digital literacy.

Requirement Level: This is driven by policy

Sources: [UNESCO](https://en.unesco.org/countries/egypt), [World Bank](https://www.worldbank.org/en/country/egypt/overview)

### 3. Content & Curriculum Alignment
Summary: The curriculum is standardized and is aligned with the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). The content is designed to develop learners' understanding and application of knowledge, skills, and values.

Requirement Level: This is driven by law and policy

Sources: [Ministry of Education](http://www.moe.gov.eg/english/)

### 4. Teacher Professional Development
Summary: Teacher professional development is a priority in Egypt. The Ministry of Education provides regular training and professional development opportunities for teachers to improve their teaching skills and integrate technology into their teaching practices.

Requirement Level: This is driven by policy and programme direction

Sources: [Ministry of Education](http://www.moe.gov.eg/english/), [World Bank](https://www.worldbank.org/en/country/egypt/overview)

### 5. Assessment Approach
Summary: The assessment approach in Egypt is based on continuous assessment and national examinations. The assessment is designed to measure learners' understanding and application of knowledge and skills.

Requirement Level: This is driven by law and policy

Sources: [Ministry of Education](http://www.moe.gov.eg/english/)

### 6. Data Governance & Privacy
Summary: Egypt's data protection and privacy laws are governed by the Personal Data Protection Law. The law provides for the protection of personal data and regulates the processing of personal data.

Requirement Level: This is driven by law

Sources: [National Telecom Regulatory Authority](http://www.tra.gov.eg/en)

### 7. Technical Interoperability
Summary: The Ministry of Communications and Information Technology (MCIT) is responsible for the country's ICT policies, including technical interoperability standards.

Requirement Level: This is driven by law and policy

Sources: [MCIT](http://www.mcit.gov.eg/)

### 8. Cybersecurity & Child Online Safety
Summary: Egypt has laws and regulations in place to protect children online and to ensure cybersecurity. The National Telecom Regulatory Authority (NTRA) is responsible for implementing these laws and regulations.

Requirement Level: This is driven by law

Sources: [National Telecom Regulatory Authority](http://www.tra.gov.eg/en)

### 9. Delivery Constraints & Design Preferences
Summary: While Egypt has made significant progress in expanding access to ICT, there are still challenges related to connectivity, especially in rural and remote areas. The government is working to address these challenges through various initiatives, such as the Egypt Vision 2030, which aims to enhance digital infrastructure and increase internet penetration.

Requirement Level: This is driven by policy and programme direction

Sources: [MCIT](http://www.mcit.gov.eg/), [Egypt Vision 2030](http://sdsegypt2030.com/?lang=en)

### 10. Notes & Gaps
- The education system in Egypt is centralized and standardized, with clear requirements for curriculum and assessment.
- There are clear national laws and regulations governing data protection and privacy, as well as cybersecurity and child online safety.
- The government has made teacher professional development a priority and provides regular training opportunities.
- There are still challenges related to connectivity, especially in rural and remote areas, which may impact the implementation of EdTech solutions.
- There is limited information available on practical implications for EdTech vendors.
