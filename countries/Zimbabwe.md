# Egypt — EdTech Suitability & Compliance Profile
A structured, source-based overview to assess how well EdTech tools align with Egypt’s education framework, digital policy environment, regulatory requirements, and delivery realities.

## Requirement Levels (narrative sections)
In sections 1–10, you may still reference whether something is grounded in *law*, *policy*, or *programme priorities*, but *do not* use colour-based indicators.

### 1. Core Pedagogy
**Summary:** Egypt's education system is governed by the Ministry of Education and focuses on a learner-centered paradigm. The curriculum is designed to develop learners' knowledge, skills, and attitudes in an integrated manner.

**Requirement Level:** Policy

**Sources:**
- [Ministry of Education](http://www.moe.gov.eg/english/)
- [UNESCO](https://en.unesco.org/countries/egypt)

### 2. Focus Skills
**Summary:** The Ministry of Education highlights critical thinking, problem-solving, and ICT skills as key competencies for learners.

**Requirement Level:** Policy

**Sources:**
- [UNICEF](https://www.unicef.org/egypt/education)

### 3. Content & Curriculum Alignment
**Summary:** EdTech tools must align with the national curriculum, covering subjects such as Arabic, Math, Science, and Social Studies.

**Requirement Level:** Law

**Sources:**
- [Ministry of Education](http://www.moe.gov.eg/english/)

### 4. Teacher Professional Development
**Summary:** The Ministry of Education provides training programs for teachers to enhance their skills in technology integration in teaching.

**Requirement Level:** Policy

**Sources:**
- [UNESCO](https://en.unesco.org/countries/egypt)

### 5. Assessment Approach
**Summary:** The education system in Egypt uses a combination of formative and summative assessments. EdTech tools should support this approach.

**Requirement Level:** Policy

**Sources:**
- [World Bank](https://www.worldbank.org/en/country/egypt)

### 6. Data Governance & Privacy
**Summary:** Egypt has enacted the Data Protection Law to regulate the processing of personal data. EdTech vendors must comply with this law.

**Requirement Level:** Law

**Sources:**
- [Data Protection Law](https://www.privacylaws.com/subjects/egypt/)

### 7. Technical Interoperability
**Summary:** The Ministry of Communications and Information Technology has established standards for e-government services. These standards must be upheld by EdTech vendors.

**Requirement Level:** Law

**Sources:**
- [Ministry of Communications and Information Technology](https://www.mcit.gov.eg/)

### 8. Cybersecurity & Child Online Safety
**Summary:** Cybersecurity laws in Egypt prioritize child online safety. EdTech tools must have mechanisms in place to ensure safety online.

**Requirement Level:** Law

**Sources:**
- [Cybersecurity Law](https://www.mcit.gov.eg/en/Strategy/Legislation/)

### 9. Delivery Constraints & Design Preferences
**Summary:** While internet penetration is high in urban areas, rural areas face connectivity challenges. EdTech tools must be designed to work in low bandwidth environments.

**Requirement Level:** Market

**Sources:**
- [World Bank](https://www.worldbank.org/en/country/egypt)

### 10. Notes & Gaps
- Clear requirements for content & curriculum alignment
- Emerging expectations for teacher professional development in ICT
- Gaps in guidance for EdTech in low bandwidth environments
- Practical implications for EdTech vendors include curriculum mapping, data processing documentation, and offline requirements

### 11. Vendor Checklist (Compact, Extractable)
A) Data protection & privacy
- ☐ Comply with Data Protection Law - Level: Must (Legal)
  
B) Cybersecurity & child online safety
- ☐ Ensure child online safety - Level: Must (Legal)

C) Curriculum & learning design alignment
- ☐ Align with national curriculum - Level: Must (Legal)

D) Teacher enablement & implementation readiness
- ☐ Support teacher training programs - Level: Expected (Market)

E) Assessment & reporting
- ☐ Support formative and summative assessments - Level: Expected (Market)

F) Interoperability & integration posture
- ☐ Comply with e-government standards - Level: Must (Legal)

G) Delivery realities (connectivity/devices)
- ☐ Design for low bandwidth environments - Level: Expected (Market)
