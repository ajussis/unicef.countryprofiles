# **Egypt — EdTech Suitability & Compliance Profile**

A structured, source-based overview to assess how well EdTech tools align with Egypt’s education framework, digital policy environment, regulatory requirements, and delivery realities.

## Requirement Levels (narrative sections)

1. **Core Pedagogy**
   - Summary: Egypt's education system has undergone significant reforms under the Egypt Vision 2030 strategy, with an aim to develop a student-centered system. The reforms emphasize improving quality, relevance, and inclusiveness in education. 
   - Requirement Level: These reforms are driven by policy.
   - Sources: [Egypt Vision 2030](http://sdsegypt2030.com/?lang=en)

2. **Focus Skills**
   - Summary: The focus in Egypt is on developing 21st-century skills including critical thinking, creativity, communication, and collaboration, along with ICT and digital literacy skills. 
   - Requirement Level: These are part of the national educational strategy.
   - Sources: [UNESCO](https://unesdoc.unesco.org/ark:/48223/pf0000370218)

3. **Content & Curriculum Alignment**
   - Summary: The curriculum in Egypt is centrally developed and schools are required to follow it. EdTech tools are expected to align with this national curriculum.  
   - Requirement Level: This is a policy requirement.
   - Sources: [UNESCO-IBE](http://www.ibe.unesco.org/sites/default/files/resources/ibe_country_report_egypt_eng_2011_0.pdf)

4. **Teacher Professional Development**
   - Summary: Egypt places a strong emphasis on teacher training, including ICT training for teachers, as part of its education reform agenda.
   - Requirement Level: This is driven by policy.
   - Sources: [World Bank](https://www.worldbank.org/en/news/press-release/2018/05/15/world-bank-supports-egypt-in-developing-first-digital-learning-platform-to-increase-access-and-improve-quality-of-education)

5. **Assessment Approach**
   - Summary: Egypt is moving towards a formative assessment approach to develop critical thinking and problem-solving skills, and away from rote learning.
   - Requirement Level: This is a policy shift.
   - Sources: [World Bank](https://www.worldbank.org/en/news/press-release/2018/05/15/world-bank-supports-egypt-in-developing-first-digital-learning-platform-to-increase-access-and-improve-quality-of-education)

6. **Data Governance & Privacy**
   - Summary: Egypt has a Personal Data Protection Law that EdTech vendors must comply with.
   - Requirement Level: This is required by law.
   - Sources: [Data Protection Law](https://www.lexology.com/library/detail.aspx?g=8e508f5f-3a04-41c0-8a8e-33c49b043ea0)

7. **Technical Interoperability**
   - Summary: The Ministry of Communications and Information Technology in Egypt is responsible for setting the technical standards and interoperability requirements.
   - Requirement Level: These are policy requirements.
   - Sources: [MCIT](http://www.mcit.gov.eg/)

8. **Cybersecurity & Child Online Safety**
   - Summary: Egypt has a cybercrime law and a child law that includes provisions for protecting children online.
   - Requirement Level: These are legal requirements.
   - Sources: [Cybercrime Law](https://www.lexology.com/library/detail.aspx?g=3a9efc9a-3727-429a-8be0-6f3e1a02a8ab), [Child Law](https://www.unicef.org/egypt/legislation_7008.html)

9. **Delivery Constraints & Design Preferences**
   - Summary: While Egypt has made significant strides in digital infrastructure, challenges remain in terms of connectivity, especially in rural areas. EdTech tools need to be designed with these constraints in mind.
   - Requirement Level: These are practical considerations.
   - Sources: [World Bank](https://www.worldbank.org/en/news/press-release/2018/05/15/world-bank-supports-egypt-in-developing-first-digital-learning-platform-to-increase-access-and-improve-quality-of-education)

10. **Notes & Gaps**
    - Clear requirements around data privacy and cybersecurity due to established laws.
    - Emerging expectations around EdTech alignment with Egypt's education reform objectives.
    - Gaps in guidance on technical interoperability standards.
    - Practical implications for EdTech vendors include designing for low connectivity and localizing to the national curriculum.
